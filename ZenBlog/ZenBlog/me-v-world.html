Me, Myself, Vs The world

Anaïs Nin, a French diarist and novelist wrote 'You don't see the world as it is but as you are'. We see it based on how our experiences and conditioning have moulded us. I'm no expert in this subject matter by the way but in nature vs nurture, I believe the latter plays a bigger role. I am on team Nurture here. We are what our parents make us, I mean our upbringing. 'We are not what we eat, but who we meet', This one is one of my favourite quotes, I recently came across it, it's by Agustín Fuentes, a Professor of Anthropology at Princeton University in his video, Why Sex, food, and shelter aren’t enough for Homo Sapiens. I am sure that at some point you've wished you could switch bodies with someone else and observe yourself from a different perspective and see if you'd find yourself interesting, or tolerable for that matter, yeah me too Haha. But truth be told, trying to see yourself from another perspective is like trying to see clearly through frosted glass, it will always be somewhat blurry.

I watch a lot of motivational videos on Instagram, mostly from @thedankoe and @richwebz, among others, I am fascinated by growth and, self-development, Philosophy, Psychology and generally making sense of the world in a deeper sensible manner. I was recently researching stuff on finding yourself and I came across this article Seeing Yourself As Others See You by Steve Fentress. He writes about the True Mirror, a phenomenon that our reflections in the regular mirrors are skewed and that we need a 'True Mirror' to see our true selves. To better understand this concept, you can watch McHugh in a TedEx talk, The Art of Being Yourself.

The idea suggests that to have a glimpse of how you look or how you appear from another person's point of view, you place two mirrors facing each other at right angles in front of you and then look at the optical illusion of the third reflection from the two mirrors. The principle behind this is that when we look into a mirror we often see our reflections reversed, that is our rights to the left and vice versa, so the second mirror undoes what the first mirror reversed and you can therefore, technically 'see yourself as others see you'. The idea is optically restoring your true image from your mirror image, letting you see yourself not just as you look, but as you really are, in real-time. You can dive deeper and learn more about it here.

I tried this with my bathroom Mirror and a backup mobile mirror and the results, were interesting. First of all and most importantly, I look even better in front of two mirrors, damn. I m kidding Haha, No I'm not I looked damn awesome Whoo. Anyway, where were we?, Yeah back to the point, the first thing I noticed is that I looked different, I mean first I smiled, did I mention I looked awesome? Haha. I mean it was somehow a different image and it sort of new in a way. If you are an Instagram and Tiktok enthusiast you must have probably tried that filter that shows you how your face would look if it was symmetrical, it is not the same principle but in both cases, you seem different. I won't dive much into this, you can try this on your own and let me know in the comments if it is the same for you.

Where am I taking you with this? Let me make this all about me for a second, I was raised in a religious setting, to Love everyone as I loved myself, not revenge, be helpful and all that pertains to a Christian home. You probably want to know how I turned out, too bad, that is not relevant. I have realized that the more I grow up, the more I question things, and nature, and the more I get to see myself, my shortcomings, and my visions. I mean you grow up and you start noticing, accepting, and trying to change some stuff about yourself with the endgame being a better version of yourself. It's a bit of a struggle but at the same time, it's an interesting journey. But let me focus on the things that I love about myself, the things that I wouldn't want any other way because I believe them to be my 'True Mirror'. To better illustrate this, let me give you a little 'Cutaway'.

I once left a shop after buying some basic household shopping. After getting home, scrolling through my M-PESA messages, I realised that the payment transaction had not gone through, I had mixed up my Sim Cards and used the one with Fuliza to pay for the items. I felt a bit guilty, called the guy back and sent the 350Ksh I owed. I mean the shop was miles away from where I lived and chances were that I'd never cross paths with the guy but I still sent the money anyway. And No, it's not because I am a good Christian, not because I was scared the guy would track me down or that I would get scammed in return, none of that. I did it because I am not that guy, it's the one threshold I wouldn't find myself crossing if I ever came crashing down, went rogue, or became a renegade (trying to make it sound cool, Haha). And it has nothing to do with the way I was raised, it's just my basic belief that you should be kind to the world and it will reciprocate. My university Philosophy Lecturer once said in a lecture that nature will always find a way to balance itself (He is an atheist by the way) and I found it insightful, I am on team nature here, Haha.

I believe that life presents everyone with great opportunities and you then decide what to do with them. Venturing into philosophical ideas and theories has got me questioning a lot of things like people's true motives, religion, society's morals etc. Most of the time, the main question that I always fail to find a genuine straight forward answer to is Who I am. I mean I have a list of things I believe about myself, things I love about myself, things I would do and those I wouldn't but still not yet able to crack this one awesome mystery. I guess it is something I will grow into but I am not going to focus on any of this today, maybe in one of my future posts.

What I am trying to mumble here is that your true self are those values or habits or traits or even morals that you'll never want to change because they are not just a part of you, they are you. Let me sound spiritual for a moment, basically, the image that shows up in the true mirror, unskewed, untainted, you are standing there and seeing this person and loving every part of him/her. What makes up a person is what they believe about themselves, and what they would want everyone else to see and believe about them (Like I want you to believe Arsenal is winning the Premiere League this season, Haha).

Peace.
